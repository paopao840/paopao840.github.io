Prologue

When the war has lasted twenty years ...

the dragonets will come.

When the land is soaked in blood and tears ...

the dragonets will come.

Find the SeaWing egg of deepest blue,

Wings of night shall come to you.

The largest egg in mountain high,

will give to you the wings of sky.

For wings of earth, search through the mud

for an egg the color of dragon blood.

And hidden alone from the rival queens,

the SandWing egg awaits unseen.

Of three queens who blister and blaze and burn,

two shall die and one shall learn

if she bows to a fate that is stronger and higher,

she'll have the power of wings of fire.

Five eggs to hatch on brightest night,

five dragons born to end the fight.

Darkness will rise to bring the light.

The dragonets are coming ...

5470 AS

That was the prophecy that ended the War of Sandwing Succession, almost 500 years ago.

A new one has been brewing. The fragile peace that has lasted the continent a few hundred years is ending, and has been for the past few decades. Border clashes and territorial disputes have been starting back up, and that have been mounting casualties.

In glacial palace of Queen Icebreaker, the Queen is in a meeting with her highest ranking general, General Snowstorm.

"Your majesty," Snowstorm started. "There has been another border incident with the Sandwings."

"Another? This is going too far. Did we suffer any casualties?" She questioned. "I feel like there was another one too, recently."

"3 critically wounded, with over a dozen with more minor wounds." The general responded, gritting his teeth. "This is the second attack this month. I believe things are going to escalate. We have to respond. Our dragons deserve better than this, getting wounded and dying for no good reason."

"I am going to send a messenger to Queen Mirage." Icebreaker explained. "If we cannot come to an agreement, I also think we need to retaliate, somehow."

"I agree." He replied. "However, I think Mirage will not be convinced easily. I will brief the other generals, and prepare a plan of action, shall negotiations fail. I believe they will. Do not get your hopes up, Icebreaker."

"Okay. You are dismissed." She replied. "I will need some time to think about this."

"Alright. Thank you, your majesty. I wish you good luck." He said, before leaving the room.

A while later, while Icebreaker was deep in thought, a knock echoed from the door.

"Mother?" A muffled voice asked.

"Yes, Polar? You may enter."

Princess Polar entered the room and stood beside her mother. "Are you thinking about something?" Polar asked. "You look distracted."

"I am." Icebreaker replied. "There have been some recent things happening between us and the Sandwings at our border. Dragons have been dying."

"Oh. I feel like Mirage is the type to not care about anyone but her own tribe. What are you going to do?" Polar asked.

"I do not know. You and Snowstorm are correct, and Mirage does not budge easily." Icebreaker explained. "I'm going to send a message to her, but I think I may be escalating this too far."

"I believe you are doing the right thing here, Mother. The dragons that died deserve fair a response against the dragons that hurt them."

"Alright. Thank you dear, I will send her an message. I hope she responds well, or we might be going to war." Icebreaker began to write.

Mirage, you need to end these attacks. Dragons are dying here. I will retaliate unless you do something about this matter at hand. This requires a quick response, before I do something. My dragons are getting hurt. They want something, and it is looking to be blood.

Icebreaker went to look for a messenger. She walking around her palace, admiring the art of the ice that formed it created. She was contemplating her message when she bumped into Blizzard, Snowstorm's son.

"Hello Blizzard, do you know what is happening with the Sandwings?" Icebreaker started. "I need to send their Queen a message."

"I think I heard him rambling about something concerning them earlier, but I don't know exactly what is going on. Why?" He asked.

"There have been a few border attacks recently, and I want to send Mirage a message so we can figure it out, preferably peacefully." She responded. "Also, I need someone to bring her a message. Do you think you could deliver it for me?"

"Of course, your majesty." He said. "I think you have probably already heard this as I overheard you speaking to my father earlier, but I doubt Mirage will care about this."

"I did, and I agree, but I think it would be a good idea to send this anyway. Even if just to try to solve it without bloodshed."

"Noble intentions, your majesty. I will try to bring this to Queen Mirage.

"Thank you, Blizzard. I wish you good luck."

She started making her way back to her chambers, and heard the talonsteps of Blizzard walking the opposite direction.

Queen Icebreaker was busying herself with work to try and distract herself from the worries nagging at the back of her mind. She has had a headache for the past few hours thinking about the situation with the Sandwings.

She was taken from her thoughts as increasingly louder talonsteps were heard.

"Your majesty, they- the Sandwings, they don't care." He growled. "They told me to get out, and that it is not their problem."

"What? Why!?" She asked. "Their dragons are dying as well, and this situation can very easily devolve into a much larger conflict!"

"I know! That's what I was trying to explain, but they just wouldn't listen." He hissed. "They were mocking me! The disrespect to the dead!"

"I hoped it would not come to this, but what almost everyone feared might happen. I can not let our dragons keep paying for the mistakes Mirage is making." She explained.

Icebreaker sighed.

"Will you have to do what I think?"

"Yes." Icebreaker paused.

"I'm going to have to declare war."
